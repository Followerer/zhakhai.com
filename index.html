The road on which we walk, is not one paved in stone or brick. The path in which we choose to tread upon is one of sand and ash, the spots where we place our feet slide away as all abandon their past to make way for the future. Yet one can never abandon their past for it clings to them, no matter how far their journey may take them from it.

The bonds people find with those they care for, these are the bonds that bring them to be the strongest, and most vulnerable. When one is brought to the point where they find themselves caught between choosing for themselves or another, it is in their nature to give their own for someone else. Zhakhai gave unto his brother a second chance, for him to live Zhakhai's life instead of his own. Yet Zhakhai had to leave to see it to fruition. Thus he left behind the path he once knew.

The first thing he noticed when he left the Azim Steppe was not the civilized nature of other people, it was the sky above. The touch of Azim's heat upon Zhakhai's skin felt...detached. The sun left a scorch upon his skin, one of uncare and unwant. It was not a heat of scorn, or a heat of acceptance. The sun seemed to forget who it was outside the Steppe.

Yet throughout his travels, at night was where his spirit was shaken. In his exile he sought to find comfort in Nhaama, to feel the kiss of moonlight against his skin as he basked in the presence of the Dusk Mother. But when the silver light slid across his skin...it felt empty yet again. He was not watched by the Mother and Father above, Zhakhai was alone and away from his people.

So it was, that he began on the path of ash and sand.

Hooks

He seems to tend to shy away from most other Xaela, and seems to almost actively avoid them.

Zhakhai seems to have an intense fascination with insects and tiny bugs.

Zhakhai Himaa seems to be a rather prolific spender, yet his clothes and weapons would lead one to believe otherwise.

Zhakhai is contracted by the Thalian Circle, a group of Sharlayan radicals who seek to contain artifacts and knowledge that were exposed when Idyllshire was abandoned.

Trivia

Zhakhai's eyes never seem to be able to focus properly, with his right eye always being more dilated than his left.

He has trouble in formal or serious occasions, seeming to constantly forget any form of appropriate mannerisms or culture.

Zhakhai has a fear of being tied down or captured in a sense, so he always wear loose torn clothing so that if someone grabs him, the clothing will tear before they can get a firm grip.

Despite his rather raggedy appearance, he seems to always smell like lavender.

Face

Unlike most of his personal effects his skin and face seem to be maintained. The grey tone which covers the surface of his features is still young and smooth, with few wrinkles. His hair is tidily kept, with most of it being drawn back into a thin gray ponytail with streaks of white whilst the rests simply points upwards. The line of his jaw runs smoothly upwards as his cheeks seem to be filled in well enough. His eyes are often found to appear relatively warm to the glance as one were to look at his blue irises surrounded by an Orange Limbus.

Body

As most other Au'Ra he stands at a rather tall height of 7'1". Despite this, he seems to be rather lanky and thin with considerable muscle still being maintained around his arms and shoulders. His skin seems to be a pale gray in appearance with scars along the back of his hands and arms. None appear to be too large except what appears to be a spot of miscolored darkened flesh on his right palm. While his skin does appear to be smooth, his hands and feet are covered in callouses from regular use.

Clothes

Zhakhai's armor appears to be a loose amalgamation of leathers and plates scrapped together in order to protect his most vital points whilst still allowing for considerable mobility. The Two Handed Sword along his back appears to be one with numerous chips and cuts into it, the leather on the handle loose and almost entirely stripped off. The clothes he chooses to wear usually are no better than his armor, mostly raggedy clothing that barely fits on his frame.
